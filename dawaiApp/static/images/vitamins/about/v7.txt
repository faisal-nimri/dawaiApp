Vitamin D:

Vitamin D is a fat-soluble vitamin that is naturally present in food and also available as a dietary supplement

Our skin produces vitamin D when sunlight strikes the skin.

Vitamin D is essential for promoting calcium absorption in the gut as it is needed for bone growth and bone structuring

It aids in cell formation , enhancing immune functioning and reducing muscles inflammation.

Vitamin D Deficiency:

Without sufficient vitamin D, bones can become thin and brittle.

Vitamin D sufficiency prevents rickets in children and osteomalacia in adults. Together with calcium, vitamin D also helps protect older adults from osteoporosis.

Symptoms are:

Bones pain.

Muscles weakness.

Dietary Sources:

Very few foods are a high source of vitamin D such as:

Fish (specially salmon and tuna.

Fish liver oils.

Beef liver.

Cheese.

Egg yolks.
