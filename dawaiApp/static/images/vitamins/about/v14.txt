Folic acid:

Folic acid is a B-vitamin that is naturally present naturally in many foods also it can be found as a supplement.

Our body needs folic acid to make DNA and other genetic materials especially during pregnancy to avoid major birth defects on baby's brain or spine it also plays a major role in iron absorption.

Folic acid deficiency is rare as needs can be met by food but if occurred due to poor dietary intake:

Loss of appetite and weight loss can occur.

Weakness.

sore tongue.

headaches.

heart palpitations.

Irritability and behavioral disorders.

Anemia.

Folic acid food Sources:

Foods with folic acid in them include leafy green vegetables, fruits, dried beans, peas and nuts. Enriched breads, cereals and other grain products also contain folic acid.
