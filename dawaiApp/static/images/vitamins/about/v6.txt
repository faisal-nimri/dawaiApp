Vitamin B12:

Vitamin B12 helps maintain healthy nerve cells and red blood cells, it's also in the synthesis of the genetic material in the cells Vitamin B12 also helps prevent a type of anemia called megaloblastic anemia that makes people tired and weak

 

Vitamin B12 is a water-soluble vitamin that is naturally present in some foods and available as a dietary supplement.
Deficiency symptoms of B12:

Health problems associated with vitamin B12 deficiency include:

Sever Anemia.

fatigue and weakness.

Constipation.

Loss of appetite and weight loss.

 

- Deficiency also can lead to neurological changes such as numbness and tingling in the hands and feet.

 

Additional symptoms of vitamin B12 deficiency are:

Difficulty in maintaining balance.

Depression and confusion.

Dementia and poor memory.

Soreness of the mouth of tongue.

 

Dietary Sources:

Vitamin B12 is naturally found in foods that come from animal sources, including:

Fish

Meat and poultry

Eggs

Milk and milk products.

Fortified breakfast cereals are particularly valuable sources of vitamin B12 for vegetarians.
