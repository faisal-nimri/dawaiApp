Vitamin B5 (Pantothenic acid):

Pantothenic acid is critical in the metabolism and synthesis of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats; also it is important in the manufacturing of red blood cells as well as aiding in maintaining a healthy digestive tract.

 

Deficiency symptoms of vitamin B5:

Pantothenic acid deficiency is rare but if happens it Symptoms are minor, including:

- Poor muscle co-ordination.

- Muscle cramps, numbness and tingling.

- Painful burning feet.

- Depression, fatigue, weakness

- Headache and loss of appetite.

 

 

Sources:

Small quantities of pantothenic acid are found in every food, but it is available in high amount in: Whole grain cereals legumes, eggs, meat, and royal jelly.
