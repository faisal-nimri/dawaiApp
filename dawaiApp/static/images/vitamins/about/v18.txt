Copper:

Copper is involved in numerous biochemical reactions in human cells it helps keeping the blood vessels, nerves, immune system, and bones healthy. Copper also aids in iron absorption, and involves in the synthesis of multiple enzymes.

Deficiency symptoms of copper:

Deficiency in humans is rare but it does occur under certain circumstances.

Symptoms of possible copper deficiency include:

Anemia and low body temperature.

Bone fractures and osteoporosis.

low white blood cell count.

Irregular heartbeat.

Increased susceptibility to infections birth defects.

Thyroid disorders.

 

Copper natural Sources:

Copper occurs naturally in many foods

Nuts and legumes.

Liver and other organs.

Meats and fish.
